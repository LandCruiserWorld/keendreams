## 1. Keen Dreams Story Summary

**The Setup: A Child's Rebellion**

Eight-year-old genius Billy Blazeâ€”having just saved Earth from the Vorticon invasion with an IQ of 314â€”sits at the dinner table facing his greatest nemesis: mashed potatoes. When Billy refuses to eat his vegetables, arguing that Earth-savers shouldn't have to, his mother unimpressed, compares him to Neil Armstrong and sends him to his room. Billy grumbles, "Stupid vegetables," and falls asleep.

**The Dream Journey**

Billy awakens on a hill, his bed surrounded by giant armed potatoes. He's been transported to Tuberia, a land ruled entirely by vegetables, courtesy of King Boobus Tuber's Dream Machineâ€”a device that kidnaps children from their dreams to serve as slaves in Castle Tuberia on Mount Tuberest. Donning his football helmet, Billy becomes Commander Keen once more. Armed with "Flower Power" instead of his usual weapons, he must navigate 40+ levels of vegetable-themed terrain, free enslaved children, and destroy the Dream Machine.

**The Resolution**

After confronting the twelve-eyed King Boobus Tuber (defeated with precisely 12 Boobus Bombs), Keen pulls a red lever on the Dream Machine. Everything wobbles, he falls asleep within the dream, and awakens back in reality. His mother then announces that the President has declared "National 'I Hate Broccoli' Day," allowing children to skip one vegetable. Billy, now emboldened by his dream adventure, declares: "Aw, mom, I'm not afraid of any stupid vegetables."

**The Meta-Game**

Keen Dreams (Episode 3.5) was the "Lost Episode," released by Softdisk in 1991 between Episodes 3 and 4. Id Software used it as a prototype testing ground for advanced features: parallax scrolling, enhanced graphics, ramps, sound card support, and new gameplay mechanics. It stands outside the main series continuityâ€”a dream episode, literally and figuratively.
